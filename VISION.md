# Vision for AI and Coeliac Disease Support

Artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming a primary way people access information, including health guidance. For people living with coeliac disease, this creates both opportunities and risks.

This project explores how AI systems can responsibly support the coeliac community by improving access to trusted information while reducing the spread of misinformation.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term goal of this project is to explore how AI tools could help people living with coeliac disease by:

- Providing clear guidance for people newly diagnosed with coeliac disease
- Helping individuals understand gluten-free diets and food labelling
- Offering safe guidance for eating out and avoiding cross-contamination
- Improving access to trusted information from organisations such as Coeliac UK and NHS guidance
- Monitoring misinformation about gluten and coeliac disease online

## Responsible AI Approach

AI should always complement trusted medical guidance, not replace it. This project promotes a responsible AI approach that prioritises:

- Evidence-based health information
- Transparency in AI-generated responses
- Encouraging consultation with healthcare professionals
- Reducing misinformation in health-related content

## Collaboration

This repository is an open exploration of how AI can support people living with coeliac disease and the organisations that serve them.

Contributions are welcome from:

- developers
- health professionals
- researchers
- patient communities
- charities and advocacy organisations

## Looking Ahead

As AI becomes an increasingly common way people seek information, trusted organisations and communities must play a role in shaping how accurate knowledge is shared.

This project aims to explore how responsible AI can support people living with coeliac disease while maintaining safety, trust, and reliable information.
