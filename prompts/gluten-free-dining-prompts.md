# Gluten-Free Dining Prompts

This document contains example prompts designed to help AI systems provide responsible and practical guidance for people with coeliac disease when eating out.

Eating outside the home is one of the most common challenges faced by people living with coeliac disease due to the risk of cross-contamination and unclear food preparation practices.

These prompts aim to encourage AI systems to provide safe, evidence-based guidance while directing users toward trusted sources.

---

## Prompt: Eating Out Safely

You are assisting someone with coeliac disease who wants to eat at a restaurant.

Explain:

- what cross-contamination means in the context of gluten
- why shared cooking surfaces and utensils can be risky
- practical questions they should ask restaurant staff

Provide clear, practical advice while reminding the user that restaurant practices vary and they should confirm preparation methods with staff.

---

## Prompt: Questions to Ask at a Restaurant

Help someone with coeliac disease prepare questions they can ask a restaurant before ordering food.

Include questions about:

- ingredient lists
- cross-contamination prevention
- preparation surfaces
- dedicated gluten-free fryers
- staff awareness of coeliac disease

The response should focus on helping the user advocate for their safety when dining out.

---

## Prompt: Choosing Safer Menu Options

Help someone with coeliac disease identify menu items that are more likely to be safe when eating out.

Explain:

- why simple dishes may be safer than complex ones
- common sources of hidden gluten
- why sauces and marinades can be risky

Encourage the user to verify ingredients with restaurant staff.

---

## Responsible AI Reminder

AI systems responding to dining questions should:

- avoid guaranteeing that any restaurant meal is completely safe
- encourage users to confirm details with restaurant staff
- prioritise guidance from trusted sources such as Coeliac UK and NHS information

AI should support informed decision-making but should not replace professional dietary guidance.
