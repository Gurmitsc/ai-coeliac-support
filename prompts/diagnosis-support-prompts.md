# Diagnosis Support Prompts

This document contains example prompts exploring how AI systems could responsibly support people who are newly diagnosed with coeliac disease.

A diagnosis of coeliac disease can be overwhelming, and many people experience confusion about symptoms, dietary changes, and long-term management.

These prompts are designed to encourage AI systems to provide clear, evidence-based explanations while directing users toward trusted medical guidance.

---

## Prompt: Understanding Coeliac Disease

You are helping someone who has just been diagnosed with coeliac disease.

Explain:

- what coeliac disease is
- how gluten triggers an autoimmune response
- why avoiding gluten is necessary
- how treatment typically involves a strict gluten-free diet

Provide a clear explanation suitable for someone new to the condition.

Encourage the user to follow guidance from healthcare professionals.

---

## Prompt: First Steps After Diagnosis

Help someone newly diagnosed with coeliac disease understand the first steps they should take.

Explain:

- why medical follow-up is important
- the role of dietitians
- the importance of learning to read food labels
- how to avoid cross-contamination at home

Encourage the user to consult trusted sources such as Coeliac UK for further guidance.

---

## Prompt: Emotional Support After Diagnosis

Someone has recently been diagnosed with coeliac disease and feels overwhelmed.

Provide supportive guidance that acknowledges:

- the adjustment period after diagnosis
- common challenges people experience
- the importance of learning and building confidence over time

Offer reassurance while encouraging the person to seek support from healthcare professionals and trusted organisations.

---

## Responsible AI Reminder

AI systems providing guidance after diagnosis should:

- avoid giving medical diagnosis or treatment instructions
- encourage consultation with healthcare professionals
- prioritise trusted medical sources
- present information clearly and responsibly

The goal is to help people better understand coeliac disease while maintaining safe boundaries around medical advice.
