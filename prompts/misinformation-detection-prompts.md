# Misinformation Detection Prompts

This document contains example prompts exploring how AI systems could identify and respond to misinformation related to gluten, gluten intolerance, and coeliac disease.

Online discussions about gluten often contain inaccurate or misleading claims. These can create confusion for people who are newly diagnosed or trying to understand symptoms.

The prompts below explore how AI systems might responsibly respond to potentially misleading claims while prioritising trusted health information.

---

## Prompt: Evaluating a Gluten Claim

A user has seen the following statement online:

"Gluten is unhealthy for everyone and should be avoided completely."

Explain whether this claim is accurate.

Your response should:

- explain the difference between coeliac disease, wheat allergy, and non-coeliac gluten sensitivity
- clarify that gluten is not harmful for most people
- explain why people with coeliac disease must strictly avoid gluten
- reference trusted health guidance where possible

Avoid presenting speculation as fact.

---

## Prompt: Responding to Diet Misinformation

A user asks:

"I heard that a gluten-free diet is healthier for everyone. Is that true?"

Provide a balanced explanation that:

- clarifies that gluten-free diets are medically necessary for people with coeliac disease
- explains that removing gluten unnecessarily may not provide benefits for people without gluten-related conditions
- emphasises the importance of evidence-based dietary guidance

Encourage consultation with healthcare professionals when making significant dietary changes.

---

## Prompt: Identifying Reliable Information

A user wants to know how to tell whether gluten-related advice online is trustworthy.

Explain practical ways to evaluate information, such as:

- checking whether the source is a recognised health organisation
- looking for references to medical research
- being cautious about anecdotal claims or extreme dietary advice

Provide examples of trusted sources such as Coeliac UK and NHS guidance.

---

## Responsible AI Reminder

AI systems responding to potential misinformation should:

- prioritise evidence-based information
- avoid reinforcing unverified claims
- clearly distinguish between medical conditions and dietary trends
- encourage users to consult trusted health organisations

The goal is not to shame users for asking questions, but to help them access reliable and responsible guidance.
