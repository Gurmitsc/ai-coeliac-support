# Trusted Sources for Coeliac Disease Information

This document lists trusted information sources that should be prioritised when designing AI systems to support people living with coeliac disease.

As AI tools increasingly become a way people access health information, it is important that responses are grounded in reliable, evidence-based guidance rather than unverified internet sources.

## Core Trusted Sources

### Coeliac UK
The leading UK charity supporting people with coeliac disease.

Provides guidance on:
- diagnosis
- gluten-free diets
- food labelling
- eating out safely
- research and advocacy

Website: https://www.coeliac.org.uk/

### NHS Guidance
UK National Health Service information on coeliac disease.

Provides medically reviewed guidance on:
- symptoms
- diagnosis
- treatment
- living with the condition

Website: https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/coeliac-disease/

### Peer-Reviewed Medical Research
AI systems supporting health questions should prioritise peer-reviewed research and evidence-based medical guidance.

Relevant areas include:
- autoimmune disease
- gastrointestinal health
- nutrition and gluten intolerance

## Responsible AI Principles

When AI systems provide information about coeliac disease they should:

1. Avoid giving medical diagnosis.
2. Encourage consultation with medical professionals.
3. Clearly distinguish between general guidance and medical advice.
4. Prioritise trusted sources such as Coeliac UK and NHS guidance.

## Purpose of This File

This repository explores how AI can responsibly support people living with coeliac disease. 

Documenting trusted sources helps ensure that AI responses are grounded in accurate, evidence-based information and reduces the risk of misinformation.
