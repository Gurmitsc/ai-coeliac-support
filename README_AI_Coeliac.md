
# AI for Coeliac Disease Support

## Overview
This open-source project explores how artificial intelligence can responsibly support people living with coeliac disease by improving access to trusted information and reducing misinformation.

AI tools are increasingly becoming a primary way people seek answers to health questions. This project investigates how trusted knowledge sources and carefully designed prompts can help ensure people receive safe, accurate and practical guidance.

The goal is not to replace medical professionals, but to help people better understand coeliac disease and navigate gluten‑free living using reliable information.

## Why This Matters
People with coeliac disease often face several challenges:

- Delayed diagnosis
- Confusing or conflicting dietary advice
- Difficulty identifying safe foods
- Anxiety around eating out or travelling
- Misinformation online about gluten intolerance and autoimmune disease

As more people turn to AI tools for answers, it becomes critical that these systems draw from trustworthy sources.

## Project Goals
This repository aims to:

- Explore responsible AI use in health-related contexts
- Provide example prompts for AI systems supporting coeliac disease education
- Highlight trusted information sources
- Encourage collaboration between technologists, charities and health communities

## Potential Use Cases
Examples of how AI could support the coeliac community include:

- Guidance for people newly diagnosed with coeliac disease
- Help interpreting gluten-free food labels
- Advice on eating out safely and avoiding cross-contamination
- Travel guidance for gluten-free living
- Summarising research or trusted health guidance

## Prompt Library
The `/prompts` directory contains example prompts designed to help AI systems respond responsibly when assisting people with questions about coeliac disease.

These prompts aim to:
- Encourage evidence-based responses
- Avoid unsafe medical advice
- Guide users toward trusted sources

## Trusted Sources
AI systems should prioritise information from credible organisations and medical research. Examples include:

- Coeliac UK
- NHS guidance
- Peer-reviewed medical literature
- Registered dietitian guidance

## Responsible AI Principles
This project follows several key principles:

1. AI should **support**, not replace, medical professionals.
2. Information should come from **trusted and verifiable sources**.
3. Systems should clearly state when users should seek **professional medical advice**.
4. Safety and accuracy should be prioritised over speed or convenience.

## Vision
As AI becomes a major channel for accessing information, trusted health organisations and communities must play a role in shaping how that knowledge is shared.

This project is an early exploration of how AI can responsibly support people living with coeliac disease and the organisations that serve them.
